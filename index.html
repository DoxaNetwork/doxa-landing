What is Doxa?
With Doxa your community can democratically select and broadcast the content that best represents your values, by using the Ethereum blockchain to transparently elect curators and reward quality effort.

Current social media is not worth your time

Overrun by cheap content

Freeing all people to be writers is a good thing, but inundating readers with low-quality content is not.

Your effort is not rewarded

Our efforts as creators, commenters, and readers have turned these sites into billion dollar companies, yet how many dollars have you received for your work?

Current social media incentivizes cheap content

Votes are too cheap

Since votes are unlimited and free to cast, users are incentivized to vote often and hastily, instead of selectively and diligently.

Voting is not compensated

Biased towards quick content

Immediate conversion of votes into visibility means what you see is not the best, but the earliest.

Community members lack control

Critical decisions about these platforms are not made by the users who want quality, but by corporations who want to sell advertisements.

You deserve better

Your time is valuable

You have only limited time, yet there is practically unlimited content on the web. You want to know if something is worth your time before you invest your time into it.

Your effort is valuable

In a world full of low-effort and misleading content, finding the best content is hard work. You want to be paid for that effort, or to pay others whom you trust to do it well.

And we can do better, together

Curation instead of moderation

positive first. confusing wording. Quality should be maintained not by searching for the bad and deleting it, but by searching for the good and promoting it.

Paid curators

Instead of each of us casting shallow votes, a trusted few should perform the effort of voting deeply, and be paid for their effort. Deep votes are quality votes, and quality votes ensure quality content.

Members own the platform

Members are able to both make the important decisions and directly share in the profit from the growth and success of their communities.

Elected curators

Curation is a powerful role, so it must be held accountable via elections powered by dynamic and continous voting, all transparently recorded on the blockchain.
